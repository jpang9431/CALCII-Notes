Instantaneous rate of change at a point
\subsection{Limit Definition of Derivatives}
$f\prime(x)=\lim_{h \to 0}{\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}}$\\
$f\prime(a)=\lim_{x \to a}{\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}}$

\subsection{Separable Differential Equation}
If $\frac{dy}{dx}=f(y)*g(x)$ than $\frac{1}{f(y)}dy=g(x)dx$\\
If $\frac{dy}{dt} = ky$ where k is a constant than $\lvert y\rvert=Ce^{kt}$\\
If $f(x)=x+y$ then $f\prime(x)=1+\frac{dy}{dx}$\\

\section{Parameterized Curves}
Curve where any point on the curve is a function of t on [a,b]: $(x=f(t),y=g(t))$ for t in $[a,b]$

\arrayrulecolor{lightgray}

Name & Formula & Notes\\
\hline
Parameterized Curves & $x=f(t), y=g(t)$ & Over t in [a,b], to find the equation solve to relate y and x removing t\\
\hline
Length of Curve & $\int_{a}^{b}\sqrt{f\prime(t)^2+g\prime(t)^2}dy$ & Divide curve into small subsections, estimate with line, sum over line, take the limit. \\
\hline
Length of Curve if $x=x$ and $y=h(x)$ & $\int_{a}^{b}\sqrt{1+h\prime(x)^2}dx$ & A special case that comes from the general formula.\\
